Theft/
Pickpocketing

Most theft incidents that happen in Ukraine are pickpocketing and take place during travel. Majority of cases happen in Kyiv metro and in/near Central Train Station.

Main mitigation measure is to use situational awareness and remember, that someone is a suitable target for a theft, if they:
* are distracted;
* openly display money or valuables;
* are intoxicated;
* leave their belongings unattended;
* are in the large crowd.

In case theft happened:
1. Call your point of contact in the country immediately. Be prepared to report exactly what was stolen: cash, bank cards, property (cell phone, digital camera, etc.) and the circumstances of the theft: place, method used and if there are any suspects.
2. Consider taking some actions for recovering the property. For instance, if the purse/wallet disappeared in a café, you may try to go there and ask the personnel if they found it.
3. If bank cards were stolen, call 24-hour bank card service center in Ukraine and/or your country bank depending on the cards origin and block them ASAP.
4. If passport and/or temporary residence permit (“posvidka”) are stolen, police report must be filed.
5. If a property that is covered with insurance was stolen police report is required for claiming insurance reimbursement.
